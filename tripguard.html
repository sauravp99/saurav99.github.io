TripGuard

Academic Project 2020

Goal

The goal of this project was to develop an application in Android Studio implemented with Kotlin. Amongst the presented cases, our team chose to work with the resources available from api.nve.no, that gave us data from the Norwegian Water Resources and Energy Directorate. The team consisted of five members; 2 programmers and 3 designers and my role was assigned to UX lead and design moderator

Research

The choice of API steered our team into a certain direction regarding choice of target group and which challenges we chose to face. The team started by exploring the API and what resources were available for us to utilize. We discovered that the API contained information on various types of natural disasters such as Avalanches, mudslides and floods and saw a potential in adapting this into our solution. After reviewing several potential target groups(truck-drivers, campers, hikers) that could have some relation to these types of disasters, our team chose to focus on Tour-goers as our target group. We proceeded to collect data to identify the different practices and habits of our target-group, we placed a focus on identifying what solutions they that preferred earlier.

Development

During the course of the next 2 months, the team went ahead with planning, developing and testing of our solution. We had chosen Kanban as our agile project management method and managed our current/remaining tasks on Trello. We utilized Github in order to collaboratively work on the application. Due to the pandemic, nearly all of our meetings were held online on Discord, which worked better than expected. During the course of our work, a project report (that reached 52 pages) was also being worked on.

Design

The design of our solution was developed togheter on Adobe XD, this was due to several of us having prior knowledge with the software. We went ahead with creating some wireframes to present to each other and eventually landed upon a final layout choice.

Solution

TripGuard is ment to ensure a safe journey for tour-goers out in nature. The application can be utilized for safely planning trips beforehand or during the trip. The user can check whether or not an area is exposed to natural disasters such as avalanches, mud slides or floods. In addition, by using the users location, the app can provide real-time notifications based on the users position.
We utilized Dokka to generate documentation for our code. The structure of our application is designed according to the Model View ViewModel which seperates the data (Model) from the UI (View) then uses the ViewModel to create a link to transfrom and provide a datastream to the view. This allowed to design the app with one single activity with several fragments Various libraries () were also used to implement nessecary functionality. In app is also responsive with a flexible UI
The project files have not yet been publiced but can be provided through request.

Reflection
